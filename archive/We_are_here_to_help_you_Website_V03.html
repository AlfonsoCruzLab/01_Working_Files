Mental Health Awareness is currently at its highest, with its day, week, and month on the calendar. Achievement by the campaigns led by public and private institutions and the hundreds of charities built around it. The shared goal is to erase the stigma around it and encourage help-seeking. But what happens when support is sought? What resources are available, and how do we access them?

We are here to help you is a series of projects where I revisit my personal experience applying for mental health aid from public services in the UK. Each through different methods of graphic communication that scan from the bigger picture of the journey to detail-orientated analyses of specific issues faced.
[Zoom = -400%]
The 1st study tracks the route I followed to access treatment through a flowchart that includes the organisations, people involved and procedures encountered across the application process. It provides a bird's-eye view of the journey and its ramifications.
[Zoom = -200%]
To look closer into the journey, we can observe the communication between the entities involved. I mapped out the interlocution between the different voices implicated, contrasting offer vs experience.
[Zoom = 100%]
The following is a printed archive that compiles the paperwork encountered across the aid application process, including forms, questionnaires, informative texts, and writing exercises. It's printed on continuous paper to embody the length - 80 m in total, longer than a 20 storey building.
[Zoom = 100%]
There's a vast number of people out there to help you. Yet, in my case, the aidees' increase seemed to have a negative impact on me. The following experiment is a first-person simulation which recreates a selection of moments I experienced.

[Zoom = 200%]
One of depression's symptoms is trouble concentrating, making the monitoring tests challenging. When we can't focus, we tend to skim, seeking specific information or the general idea of the content. I ran the PHQ-9 through a distilling system, removing words without altering the text's meaning, emulating how someone struggling to focus might read it.
[Zoom = ∞]
This project aims to provide an insight into what happens after reaching out for help. The above studies use my experience as the focal point; now, let's shift the axis to different angles to expand.
