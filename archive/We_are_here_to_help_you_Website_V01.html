Mental Health Awareness is currently at its highest, with its day, week, and month on the calendar. Achievement by the campaigns led by public and private institutions and the hundreds of charities built around it. The shared goal seems to erase the stigma around it and encourage help-seeking. But what happens when support is sought? What resources are available, and how do we access them?

We are here to help you is a series of projects where I revisit my personal experience applying for mental health aid. Each through different methods of graphic communication to scan from the bigger picture of the journey to detail-orientated analyses of specificities faced.

Reflective Research through Graphic Communication
Even though these projects are public, the nature of the research is introspective. I'm looking back at a personal experience in an attempt to process it - in a similar way to how psychotherapy deals with trauma. Doing it through different lenses nourishes a broader view, enabling a clearer understanding of what happened and why it felt the way it did.
