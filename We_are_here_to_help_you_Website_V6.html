Mental health awareness is currently at its highest, marked by days, weeks and months on the calendar. This is the achievement of the many campaigns led by public and private institutions, and the hundreds of charities built around these issues. The shared goal is to erase the stigma around mental health disorders and encourage people to seek help. But what happens when help is sought? What resources are available, and how do we access them?

We are here to help you is a series of projects in which I revisit my personal experiences applying for mental health aid from public services in the UK. Each is presented through different methods of graphic communication design that scale from the bigger picture of the journey down to detail-oriented analyses of specific encounters.

The first analysis tracks the route I followed to access treatment through a flowchart that includes the organisations, people and procedures encountered across the application process. It provides a bird's-eye view of the journey and its ramifications.

The following is a printed archive that compiles the paperwork encountered across the aid application process, including forms, questionnaires, informative texts and writing exercises. It's printed on continuous paper to embody the length of this procedure - 80m in total, taller than a 20-story building.

The questionnaires used to monitor mental health appear at most stages of the journey. Over time, I found them challenging to complete, possibly because one of the symptoms of depression is trouble concentrating. When we can't focus, we tend to skim-read, searching for specific information or the general idea of the content. The following distilling system emulates how someone struggling to focus might read one of the tests.
